# A History of Sludge

It all started many many years ago when a little boy and his sister were wandering at some desolate festival and out of nowhere heard some Black Sabbath-like tunes. They decided to take a look, it was a very impressive Thee Plague Of Gentlemen playing. Not much later the ‘Dreams Of What Life Could Have Been’-compilation came into his life and the little boy got completely sold to this genre of music.
Nobody can remember how he got to know Möse but anyway he booked them to play for a handful of locals in a youth center. They had a chat and gave him a free copy of a sampler/compilation. Whilst skimming this sampler/compilation at his humble home, he had his first encounter with the term "Doom It Yourself" and saw it popping up more and more as time went by (article on HDDT, name of Gura's latest record label, etc).
Which, relying on its context, convinced him it was the right name for a world changing project.

# Do-It-Yourself

Since the 80’s hardcore punk bands from all over the world have been able to tour and release material through an international DIY network. This network is made out of musicians, bands, concert promoters and record producers/studios in which favors are exchanged instead of monetary payment, which has resulted into an independent, vibrant and more pure musical environment.
Lately this mindset has been extended to even larger projects. A Danish association (UMF) has organized two years in a row successful festivals based on DIY ethics.
The purpose of Doom-It-Yourself is to organize a similar project in Belgium. In practice this will mean that bands will perform in exchange for a place to sleep, food, drinks and the transportation costs. The performances will be held over at multiple venues/squats sympathetic to our idea, like KRAAK has been doing in Belgium for so many years.
The project is entirely based on the work of volunteers, which will subsequently mean that profits will be invested into upcoming projects.

# Corporate Music Industry

In the days where so-called ‘alternative’ venues are owned by multinational sharks like MTV, where music festivals (of which many originally were meant as a protest against corporate culture) are now hosted by Rich Uncle Pennybags, the need for underground counter-culture continues to grow.
With the recent government cuts in the cultural budget the underground music scene in Belgium has suffered casualties. This setback has left us with a need to look into different ideas to grow. This while being more self-reliant at the same time.

# Open-Source Project

An open-source project/ecosystem is not about throwing together a pile of code and slapping a copyleft license on top of it. It’s about a superior software development model (given equal manpower) where you foster reuse and collaboration with other open-source projects. It is about being a living cell of an organism, an active part of an ecosystem.
When you contribute to open-source projects that have been doing things “the open-source way” (that is: depending on shared libraries and contributing to them—be it testing or providing fixes upstream), you end up fostering the use and improvement of the technologies that are the heart and soul of your open-source desktop/operating system.
In the long run, you end up improving widgets, frameworks, engines, libraries, icon themes and artwork… the list goes on.
You don't have to worry about screwed up licensing, too much control over the direction of the project, copyright assignment clauses, “read-only” sources, etc.
Now consider this: the open-source concept doesn’t have to just apply to software. It can apply to anything in life, any area where information is currently in the hands of few instead of many, any area where a few people control the production and distribution and improvement of a product or service or entity.
Open-source is helping of the community, instead of government welfare.
